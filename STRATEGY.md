# HI Leads — Outreach Strategy v1 (2026-05-23)

Author: Artemis (counsel-reviewed). Audience: Austin + Peter Higney.

## Core thesis

Peter face-to-face is the closer. The whole strategy is **manufacturing chokepoints that put Peter in the same room as named decision-makers, with the minimum amount of travel time per qualified meeting.**

Cold email + LinkedIn DM has near-zero conversion at this seniority level. What converts is: industry-peer credibility (Peter), physical samples in the room, and a designer or operator who already trusts you walking you into the next conversation.

So the strategy is a sequence of physical events, not a campaign of messages.

## Layer 2 — Designer-first wedge (parallel to trips)

For every brand on the list, the architect/designer of record is the lower-resistance entry point. Designers are flattered when a furniture maker shows up at their studio with hard samples. VPs of Design at chains are gatekept; designers welcome new vendors because new vendors give them new options to specify.

**Top 5 designer firms to pursue directly:**
1. **Aria Group Architects** (Chicago) — Cooper's Hawk + 50+ other restaurants. Highest leverage.
2. **Studio Murnane** (Phoenix) — FRC universe.
3. **Meyer Davis Studio** (NYC) — 1 Hotels, luxury hospitality.
4. **NELSON Worldwide** — Velvet Taco prototype + many other brands.
5. **Shepley Bulfinch** (Phoenix office) — Postino + Upward Projects.

**Move per firm:** request a 20-min sample drop at their studio. Bring physical samples (not catalogs). Ask one specific question: "What's currently underspec'd on your last three restaurant builds?" Listen. Send a follow-up sample matched to their answer.

## Layer 3 — Industry event play (annual rhythm)

Get Peter on the floor at these. He doesn't pitch — he peers.

- **BDNY (Boutique Design NY)** — November, Javits Center. Hospitality design buyers, 1 Hotels / Auberge / Soho House territory. Peter walks the floor, sets up booth meetings in advance via LinkedIn.
- **HD Expo + Conference** — May, Las Vegas. Hospitality design industry. Sam Fox has spoken here. Postino, FRC, hotel groups all attend.
- **RestaurantSpaces** — fall (Phoenix usually). Smaller, executive-only. Marji Aron and her peers are there. Highest-density event for restaurant dev VPs.
- **Maggiano's reunion / Brinker alumni circuit** — informal but powerful. Whoever opened the Maggiano's door for HI is the same generation as half these decision-makers. Ask Peter who he should re-introduce HI to from his Brinker-era network.

Pick ONE event for 2026 to commit. Walking BDNY with no plan is wasted. Walking BDNY with 6 pre-booked 15-min booth meetings is the trip.

## Layer 4 — Creative moves (the unconventional plays)

These are the ones cold-email playbooks miss.

### 1. The HI Showroom Dinner
Build a half-day in Fort Lauderdale: factory tour + dinner with Peter at a Higney-favorite restaurant. Fly designers (not VPs) in on HI's dime. Cap at 8 attendees. Invite Aria Group, Studio Murnane, NELSON, Meyer Davis principals. Two of them will become repeat specifiers within 12 months. Cost: ~$8K. ROI: every project those firms touch for the next decade.

### 2. The Active-Build Drop-In
Known confirmed-in-build sites this year:
- North Italia Katy TX (opens June 10 — June 10 is too late, target last 2 weeks of May / first week June for site visit)
- bartaco One Loudoun (in build now)
- bartaco North Bethesda (opened Feb 2026 — already done, but the bartaco DC and Chicago locations are permit-stage now)
- Earls Boston Seaport (2027 — too early)
Peter shows up at the active site, asks for the GC, hands them a card and a sample. The GC tells the designer "this guy showed up with real wood." Costs almost nothing. High signal.

### 3. The Lookbook Hand-Delivery
Print a professional hard-copy lookbook of completed HI projects — Nikki Beach, Hyatt Place Boca, Joia, Boatyard. Foil-stamped cover. Peter never goes to a meeting without one. Costs ~$40/unit. Print 50. Decision-makers DO keep good lookbooks on their shelf; they do not keep PDFs.

### 4. The Maggiano's Tear-Sheet
The reason these leads are on the list is the Maggiano's framework. Turn the Maggiano's case into a one-page tear-sheet: photo + spec + the operating cost data Peter has. That sheet does more in a 5-minute meeting than 30 slides.

### 5. The "Specified Vendor" Quiet Goal
For each Trip A/B/C, the explicit objective is NOT to close a brand. It's to get one designer firm to add HI to their internal specified-vendor list. That's a slow asset — once HI is on Aria Group's spec list, every Aria client becomes an HI lead automatically for years.

### 6. The Architect Referral Bonus
Offer architects/designers a 1–2% referral fee on the first project per specified-by relationship. Most designers can accept this if structured as a "design integration support fee" rather than a kickback. Two firms saying yes pays for itself in one project.

### 7. The Sam Fox 5-Minute Move
Sam Fox is design-obsessed and accessible at industry events. If Peter can get 5 minutes with Sam at HD Expo or via a warm intro, he doesn't pitch — he asks ONE question: "What's one material on your current spec sheet you'd replace if you could?" That answer, if Peter responds within 48 hours with a sample of the alternative, opens the whole FRC universe.

## Sequencing — the next 90 days

**Weeks 1–2:** Build the lookbook. Print 50 units. Build the Maggiano's tear-sheet. Get Peter's existing-network list of who-to-reintroduce.

**Weeks 3–4:** Designer-first outreach (warm). Email Aria Group, Studio Murnane, Meyer Davis, NELSON, Shepley Bulfinch with a single line: "Peter Higney (Higney International, Fort Lauderdale) will be in [your city] week of [date]. 20 min for a sample drop?" No pitch in the email.

**Weeks 5–8:** Trip A — Phoenix. Anchor on Marji Aron meeting (cold ask via FRC dev office). Pad with Studio Murnane, Postino, True Food, one GC walkthrough.

**Weeks 9–12:** Trip B — Chicago. Anchor on Aria Group. Pad with Cooper's Hawk, Lettuce Entertain You, Parker.

**Weeks 13+:** Trip C — NYC/Boston. By this point Trips A and B have surfaced which targets are real and which are dry. Trip C is built around whichever introductions came out of A and B.
